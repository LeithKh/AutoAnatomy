Can't disengage the clutch

  • Clutch contaminated with oil. Remove the clutch driven disc and inspect.
  • The driven disk is deformed or damaged.
  • Fatigue loss of elasticity of the diaphragm spring. Remove the driven disk/pressure plate assembly and inspect.
  • Leak in the clutch hydraulic system. Check the master cylinder, slave cylinder and lines.
  • Air in the clutch hydraulic system. Remove air from the system.
  • Insufficient pedal travel. Check and adjust.
  • The piston seal in the working cylinder is deformed or damaged.
  • Lack of grease on the guide bushing.

The clutch is slipping

  • The driven disc is worn or has oil on it.
  • The driven disk has not run in. It may take 30 to 40 normal engagements to break in a new clutch.
  • The diaphragm spring is weakened or damaged. Remove the driven disk/pressure plate assembly and inspect.
  • Flywheel is deformed.
  • Metal particles in the master cylinder prevent the piston from returning to its normal position.
  • Clutch hydraulic line is damaged.

Vibration when engaging clutch

  • There is oil on the driven disc. Remove the disk and inspect. Eliminate the cause of the oil leak.
  • Engine or transmission mounts are worn or loose. These units may move slightly when the clutch is disengaged.
  • Inspect the fasteners and bolts.
  • The splines on the gearbox input shaft are worn. Remove the clutch parts and inspect.
  • The pressure plate or flywheel is deformed. Remove the clutch parts and inspect.
  • Fatigue loss of elasticity of a diaphragm spring. Remove the clutch cover and pressure plate assembly and inspect.
  • Hardening or deformation of clutch linings.
  • Clutch lining rivets are loose.

Squealing or rattling sounds are heard when the clutch is fully engaged

    Squealing or rumbling sounds are heard when the clutch is fully engaged (pedal released)

    • Incorrect pedal adjustment. Adjust the pedal free play.
    • Loosen the bearing fastening on the gearbox shaft. Remove the clutch elements and inspect the bearing.Remove any burrs or nicks; Clean and lubricate again before reinstalling.
    • The guide bushing is worn or deformed.
    • Clutch lining rivets are loose.
    • There are cracks on the clutch driven disc.
    • Fatigue of torsion springs of the driven clutch disc. Replace the driven disk.

    When the clutch is completely disengaged (the pedal is pressed), squealing or rumbling sounds are heard

    • Worn, defective or broken clutch release bearing.
    • Worn or broken sectors of the pressure plate diaphragm spring.

Clutch pedal stays on floor after release

  • Sticking of clutch levers and rods or clutch release bearing.
  • Inspect the clutch levers and rods or remove the clutch elements.

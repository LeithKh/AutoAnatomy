Low clutch release force

  • The clutch cable is torn.
  • Broken release bearing and its fork.

Unclear gear shifting

  • Gearbox defect.
  • Clutch disc defective.
  • Incorrect assembly of the fork/release bearing assembly.
  • Defective pressure plate.
  • Loosening of the clutch basket to the flywheel.

Clutch slipping

  • Worn clutch disc.
  • Disc slipping due to crankshaft oil seal leak.
  • Insufficient clutch disc seating.
  • Warped pressure plate or flywheel.
  • Weak diaphragm springs.
  • Clutch disc overheating.

Selections when engaging the clutch

  • Oily or burning clutch disc.
  • Worn or loose engine or gearbox mounts.
  • Worn clutch disc hub splines.
  • Warped pressure plate or flywheel.
  • Burning or tarring of the flywheel or pressure plate.

Rattling in the gearbox

  • Worn release bearing fork.
  • Defective clutch disc damper springs.
  • Low engine idle speed.

Noise in the clutch area

  • Incorrect fork shaft installation.
  • Bearing failure.

The clutch pedal does not return to its original position

  • Clutch cable is faulty.
  • Broken fork or release bearing.

High force on the clutch pedal

  • Kink in the cable or levers.
  • Pressure plate malfunction.
  • The main and slave hydraulic cylinders installed are not suitable for this vehicle.

Impact noises at low speeds

  • Wear of CV joints of drive axles.
  • Wear of the differential side gear shaft.

Clunking sound when accelerating and decelerating

  • Worn engine or gearbox mounts.
  • Worn main gear drive shaft.
  • Wear of the differential side gear shaft.
  • Worn or damaged CV joints.

Clicking sound when turning

  • Wear or damage to CV joints (external).

Vibration

  • Damage to wheel bearings./li>
  • Damage to drive axles.
  • Out of roundness of tires.
  • Wheels are not balanced.
  • Wear of CV joints.

Noise in one particular gear

  • Damage or wear of the constant mesh gear.
  • Damage or wear to the synchronizers.
  • The reverse gear shift fork is bent.
  • The 4th gear is damaged.
  • Reverse gear is worn or damaged.

Noise in all gears

  • Insufficient lubrication.
  • Worn or damaged bearings.
  • Worn or damaged primary and/or secondary shafts.

Switching off gears

  • Worn or incorrectly adjusted rods.
  • Loss of attachment of the box to the engine.
  • Gear shift shafts are bent.
  • The input shaft bearing retainer is lost or broken.
  • Dirt between the clutch cover and the flywheel housing.
  • Worn shift fork.

Oil leaks

  • Excess oil in the box.
  • Wear of differential seals.
  • The input shaft bearing retainer is lost or broken.
  • Damage to the input shaft seal.

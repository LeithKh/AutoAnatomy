Engine doesnt turn when trying to start

  • Battery contacts are loose or corroded.
  • The battery is low or damaged.
  • The clutch pedal is not fully depressed, contact is lost in the starter control circuit, the starter gear is jammed by the flywheel.
  • Starter relay faulty.
  • Starter malfunction.
  • Malfunction of the ignition switch.
  • Malfunction of the ignition switch.
  • Breakage of starter gear or flywheel teeth.

Engine turns over but doesnt start

  • There is no fuel in the tank.
  • Low starting speed (battery is discharged).
  • Poor contact at battery terminals.
  • Leaks in injectors, malfunction of the carburetor, fuel pump, pressure regulator.
  • Fuel does not fit into the carburetor or fuel injector rail.
  • Damage to ignition system elements.
  • Worn or incorrectly adjusted spark plug electrodes.
  • Loss of contacts in the ignition system.
  • Incorrect ignition timing adjustment.
  • Ignition coil malfunction.

Engine coldstart issues

  • The battery is low.
  • Incorrect operation of the fuel injection system.
  • Faulty starting injector.
  • Leaks from injectors.
  • Defective distributor cap.

Engine hotstart issues

  • The air filter is clogged.
  • No fuel is supplied.
  • The battery contacts are oxidized, especially the “mass” one.

Unusual engine starter noise

  • Broken starter or flywheel gear teeth. The starter
  • mounting bolts have been loosened.

Engine starts then stalls

  • Disadvantages in the electrical connections of the distributor, coil or generator.
  • Insufficient fuel supply - check the operation of the fuel pump or whether the fuel pumps are blocked tubes
  • Air leaking into the carburetor or intake manifold.
  • Check all connections and vacuum hoses.

Oily engine

  • Oil leaks through the oil pan gasket, valve cover, engine seals, etc.

Idle hunting

  • Vacuum leaks.
  • Check the condition of the vacuum hoses.
  • Exhaust gas recirculation valve is loose.
  • The air filter is clogged.
  • Insufficient fuel supply.
  • Opening the gas joint of the cylinder head.
  • Worn camshaft drive belt.
  • Camshaft cam wear.
  • Malfunction of the carburetor or injection system.

Engine missfire under load

  • The fuel filter is clogged.
  • Low fuel consumption through injectors.
  • Damage to spark plugs.
  • Incorrect ignition timing setting.
  • The distributor cap is cracked or its contacts are damaged.
  • Leaks in high voltage wires.
  • Incorrect functioning of the EGR system.
  • Insufficient compression pressure.
  • Ignition system malfunction.
  • Vacuum leaks.

Engine RPM drops when accelerating

  • Spark plugs are faulty.
  • The carburetor or injection system is not adjusted.
  • The fuel filter is clogged.
  • Incorrect ignition timing setting.
  • Vacuum leaks.
  • Defect in high-voltage wires or other components of the ignition system.

Engine works in an unstable manner

  • Vacuum leaks.
  • Fuel pump defective.
  • Loss of contact in the injector connector.
  • Defective electronic control module.

Engine stops on its own

  • Faulty idle speed control.
  • There is water in the fuel or the fuel filter is clogged.
  • Damage to the distributor.
  • EGR system defect.
  • Defective spark plugs.
  • Defect in high voltage wires.
  • Vacuum leaks.
  • Incorrect valve clearance adjustment.
  • Fuel system defect.

Engine backfire

  • Incorrect operation of the EGR system.
  • Incorrect ignition timing setting.
  • Defect in the ignition system (cracks in the spark plug insulator, high-voltage wires, distributor cap).
  • Incorrect fuel system adjustment.
  • Vacuum leak.
  • Incorrect adjustment of valve clearances, stuck or burnt valves.

Engine detonation noises when accelerating

  • Low quality fuel.
  • Incorrect ignition timing setting.
  • Incorrect fuel system adjustment.
  • Damage to spark plugs or high-voltage wires.
  • Worn or damaged distributor components.
  • EGR system defect.
  • Vacuum leaks.
  • Coal deposits (carbon deposits) in the combustion chamber.

Low engine oil pressure

  • Low oil level or low oil viscosity.
  • Low idle speed.
  • Short circuit.
  • Defective oil pressure sensor.
  • Worn bearings and/or oil pump.

Battery running flat

  • Alternator drive belt defective.
  • Low electrolyte level.
  • Battery contacts are oxidized.
  • Low charging current of the generator.
  • Malfunctions in the electrical circuit.
  • Short circuit in the wiring.
  • Internal battery defect.

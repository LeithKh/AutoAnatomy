The car pulls to the side when braking

  • Incorrect tire pressure, different types of tires on the same axle.
  • Increased resistance (for example, kinking) of brake pipes andhoses.
  • The brake drum or shoe is not functioning properly.
  • Parts of the suspension are missing.
  • The brake shoe is lost, the linings are worn on one side.

Noise when braking

  • Pad wear. Replace them with new ones immediately.

Pulsation of force on the brake pedal

  • Increased drum or disc beat.
  • Uneven pad wear.
  • Defective brake discs.

Increased braking force

  • The brake booster is faulty.
  • The brake force redistribution system is faulty.
  • Pad wear.
  • Brake cylinder stuck.
  • Oiling of brake pads.
  • New pads that have not broken in.

Increased brake pedal travel

  • Incorrect functioning of the brake force redistribution system.
  • There is little brake fluid in the master cylinder.
  • Air in the brake system.

Brake delay

  • Incorrect adjustment of the brake light switch.
  • The master cylinder piston does not return completely.
  • Resistance in brake pipes and hoses, e.g. due to kinking.
  • Incorrect parking brake cable adjustment.

Sticking and insufficient brake action

  • Malfunctions of the brake force redistribution system.
  • Brake booster malfunction.
  • There is a bend in the pedal drive mechanism.

Variable brake pedal pressure

  • Air in the system.
  • Loss of tightening of the master cylinder mounting bolts.
  • Master brake cylinder defective.
  • Master brake cylinder defective.
  • Low pressure on the brake pedal.
  • Low fluid level in the master cylinder reservoir due to leaks through the brake cylinders.
  • Damage to brake pipes.

Suspension and steering faulty

  • the tires are not worn out and have normal pressure.
  • the steering shaft cardan mounting is correct.
  • there is no damage to the suspension and steering mechanism.
  • The wheels are balanced and the bearings are serviceable.

The car pulls to the side

  • Different tires on one axle.
  • Broken or damaged springs.
  • Incorrect wheel alignment.
  • Front brake sticking.

Increased tire wear

  • Incorrect wheel alignment.
  • Broken or sagging springs.
  • Wheels are unbalanced.
  • Shock absorber failure.
  • Constant vehicle overload.
  • Increased noise from wheels.
  • Tire defect.
  • Shock absorber defect.

Wheel vibration

  • Wheels are unbalanced or out of round.
  • Bearing wear.
  • Worn tie rod ends.
  • Wear of ball joints.
  • Increased wheel runout.
  • Tire defect.

Increased effort on the steering wheel

  • There is no lubrication in the ball joints, tie rod end and steering gear.
  • Incorrect wheel alignment.
  • Low tire pressure.

Steering does not return to straight ahead position

  • There is no lubrication in the ball joints.
  • Ball joints are bent.
  • The steering column is bent.
  • There is no lubrication in the steering mechanism.
  • Incorrect wheel alignment.
  • Damage to steering or suspension components.

Increased noise from the front of the car

  • There is no lubrication in the ball joints.
  • Damage to the rack mounting.
  • Worn tie rod bushings or tie rod ends.
  • Loosening of the stabilizer.
  • Loosening the wheel nuts.
  • Loose suspension fastening.

Poor steering stability

  • There is no lubrication in the ball joints.
  • Ball joints are bent.
  • The steering column is bent.
  • There is no lubrication in the steering mechanism.
  • Incorrect wheel alignment.
  • Damage to steering or suspension components.

Steering wheel shakes when braking

  • Worn wheel bearings.
  • Broken or sagging springs, leaking wheel brake cylinders.
  • Warping of the brake drum or disc.

Excessive roll when cornering and braking

  • Stabilizer defect.
  • Shock absorber mounts are worn or defective.
  • Broken or sagging springs.
  • Vehicle overload.

Patchy tire wear

  • The wheel is unbalanced.
  • Disk damage.
  • Tire defect.
  • Increased clearances in the steering.
  • Bearing wear.
  • Wear of the tie rod ends.
  • Breakage of the drive gear or steering rack.
  • Wear of the intermediate shaft.
